# Code of Conduct

## The short version

Be decent. Assume the other person is trying to help.

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for
everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability,
ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of
experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance,
race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our standards

Behaviour that keeps this project worth contributing to:

- Assuming good faith, and asking before concluding
- Giving and accepting constructive feedback — on the code, not the person
- Saying "I was wrong" when you were, which is a normal part of engineering here:
  a five-way audit of this repository found it claiming more than it knew, and
  the fixes are in the changelog under my own name
- Respecting that people contribute in different languages and time zones, and
  that English is not everyone's first language

Behaviour that is not acceptable:

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks — public or private
- Sexualised language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention
- Publishing someone's private information without their explicit permission
- Sustained disruption of discussions
- Any other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting

## Security reports are held to a different standard, deliberately

If you find a way past the read-only guard, that is a **contribution**, not an
attack, and it will be treated as one. See [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) for how to
report it privately. Nobody has ever been made unwelcome here for proving the
project wrong — that is the job.

## Scope

This applies in all project spaces (issues, pull requests, discussions, commit
messages) and when someone is representing the project in public.

## Enforcement

Report unacceptable behaviour to the maintainer via **GitHub → Security → Report
a vulnerability** (which is private) or by opening an issue if the matter is not
sensitive. All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly, and
the reporter's privacy and security will be respected.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this code in good faith may face
temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by other maintainers.
